Michael Jackson's "Billie Jean," released in 1983 on his album Thriller, is one of the most iconic pop songs of all time. The song is built around a distinctive bassline and a haunting story about a woman named Billie Jean, who claims that Jackson is the father of her child. Inspired by the real-life experiences of Jackson and his brothers, who often encountered fan obsessions and false paternity claims, the song explores themes of fame, deceit, and vulnerability. The track not only topped global charts but also redefined pop music with its blend of funk, R&B, and rock elements. Jackson’s moonwalk debut during the song's live performance on Motown 25 solidified both the song and the artist’s legacy in music history.>
